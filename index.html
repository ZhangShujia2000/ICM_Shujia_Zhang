Summary  
A system of higher education plays a very signiﬁcant role in a country. It acts as not only an  
industry but also a main source of trained and educated citizens for the nation’s economy. However,  
the system of higher education of many countries still has a lot of room for development. Having a  
healthier and more sustainable system of higher education is what every country wants. Thus, the  
world needs an evaluation model urgently.  
We ﬁrst establish a Multi-level Fuzzy Comprehensive Evaluation (MFCE) Model for the health  
of the system of higher education. By analyzing the data of 50 countries, the indicators of the  
model are divided into 3 layers. After that, we use the entropy weight method (EWM) to obtain  
the weight matrix of the three-layers indicators by Matlab. Then, we divide the data range of each  
indicator in the 50 countries into 5 segments, that is, into 5 levels to build a comment set. Finally,  
we get the membership function. To validate the model, we re-select 6 countries and apply the  
model to measure the health of their systems of higher education.  
Based on the evaluation results of our model, our team believes that Japan is a country with  
room for development. After analyzing the current situation in Japan, we put forward a reasonable  
and achievable vision for it. In the vision, we set speciﬁc data for the goal based on our evaluation  
model. Next, we apply our model to measure the health of the system in the vision, and got the  
corresponding results.  
Then we propose a Three-steps policy to support the proposed vision. On the basis of our  
policy, we establish a model to estimate the economic impact on Japan, predicting the percentage  
of education investment in Japan in the next 50 years. The results show that our policy is brilliant  
for Japan.  
Moreover, we analyze the results of our model and evaluate it objectively to help us improve our  
policy. Combining with the actual situation, we explain the necessity and strengths of implement  
our policy. In addition, we discuss the real-world impacts of implementing our plan both during  
the transition and in the end state. In view of the real situation, we acknowledge that it is quite  
diﬃcult to make such a huge change.  
At the end of our paper, we make sensitivity analysis, and the result of which proves that our  
model has a good stability. Besides, we analyze the advantages and disadvantages of the model,  
which will help us to further optimize the model.  
Keywords: Higher Education; Policies and Implementations; MFCE; EWM

1 Introduction  
1.1 Background  
The higher education system occupies an unshakable position in the construction of countries  
around the world. For individuals, higher education is a direct way to enhance self-worth. For  
society and nation,it has value both as an industry itself and as a source of trained and educated  
citizens for the nations economy.Each country has its own unique way of higher education.  
In the upsurge of globalization, hundreds of millions of international students have experienced  
a variety of national approaches to higher education. The current pandemic has brought certain  
adjustments to the higher education systems of various countries, and has also begun to make  
countries reﬂect on the advantages and disadvantages of the existing systems. Having a healthier  
and more sustainable higher education system is what every country wants.

even potential eﬀects. We only consider factors with visible weight.

6.2.1 the Requirement of Comprehensive Development  
A healthy and sustainable higher education system does not refer to a single-sided and aggressive  
development of education and a state of deviation from its focus, nor is it a way of only developing  
the economy, but a virtuous cycle model that can be applied for a long time. Here we propose  
that the car model corresponds to the structural system corresponding to national development. A  
countrys economy, science, technology, social culture and other aspects are important components  
of a country. The question of how powerful a country can be is that of how far a car can go.  
Economy is the engine. Electrical appliances are science and technology. The chassis is the  
social "culture". And the body is education. A short board in a part means "unhealthy", and an  
incomplete or weak part means "not continuous". Each component is related to each other, and  
each has a diﬀerent function and mutual inﬂuence. Furthermore, it turns out that there are complex  
connections between various structures. As shown in the ﬁgure above, higher education is an  
important segment in national development, not unique and independent, and its relationship with  
other parts should not be underestimated.

7 Sensitivity analysis  
Judging from the overall eﬀect of our model, it is not only ﬁt for the environment and area  
mentioned in the paper but also suited to most ﬁelds related to refugees, that is, our model has  
relatively wide range of applicability.  
In addition, our model does not rely on certain speciﬁc data. As shown in Task 4, when the  
original data changes, the membership of our model results will change. When we change some  
data in the model, the membership of the model will ﬂuctuate greatly. Therefore, the level of health  
of the national higher education system will also change to some extent, which indicates good  
sensitivity and weak stability of our model.

8 Strengths and Weaknesses  
8.1 Strengths  
• Accurate  
The higher education system does not have a decisive value to assess. It means that the  
evaluation of the higher education system is dynamic and uncertain, that is, it appears to be  
vague. Therefore, our Fuzzy Comprehensive Evaluation Model can describe it better. The  
Entropy Weight Method is objective, so the result is more accurate.  
• Applies widely  
This system can be used for any countries in the world. Once we collect enough data, we can  
assess the health and sustainability of higher education system in a nation.  
• Close to the reality  
We consider 13 factors and combine Entropy Weight Method with Fuzzy Comprehensive  
Evaluation Method to get an evaluation model. The Health Model is found to be basically  
realistic when applied to six countries in this paper.  
8.2 Weaknesses  
• The decision of the evaluation level is subjective. Although a lot of research has been done,  
this model will not be so convincing because there is no deﬁnite evaluation standard for the  
higher education system.  
• If you continue to expand the indicator set U, super-blurring may occur, which means it is  
impossible to distinguish who has a higher membership.
